Lost & Found

The lost & found project is designed and proposed to be a potential new feature for Waze, an app providing real-time driving directions with live traffic updates. This feature allows Waze users to report their lost and/or found personal items and then mark them on the in-app real-time interactive map, which dramatically improves efficiency and accuracy compared to the current situation.

Futurator - a Redesign of refrigerator

people liked this project

Food waste is a widespread and increasingly urgent global problem. According to research in 2011, the Food and Agriculture Organization estimates approximately one third of food produced for human consumption, is lost or wasted annually. The threat of food waste for our sustainable future is increasingly being recognized by many countries and organizations. UN targets a 50% reduction in consumer food waste and the EU recycling of 60% of all household waste, both by 2030. In 2015, the U.S. 2030 Food Loss and Waste Reduction goal has also been announced. Led by the EPA, USDA and FDA, this is the first-ever domestic goal to reduce food loss and waste, which seeks to cut food loss and waste in half by the year 2030. We know that refrigerator is the major place for food storage at consumers' home. However, as a designer, I found it is the place where many foods have been wasted simultaneously. Extant refrigerator products are very similar to each other: They are visually closed; They are always boxy so not easy to check and get food in the dark and deep corners; Their rooms and shelves are preset, even though some of them have 'flex zone' as advertised, users can not freely adjust it based on their own needs. What if we make a new refrigerator with transparent doors so users can clearly see what they have inside? What if a refrigerator is in cylinder, not boxy anymore, so the user can put in and retrieve food easier? What if the whole storage room can be adjusted, not only the size but also the temperature for different foods, freely by the consumers? Even from a phone? In this project, I'm going to discover these possible design opportunities, to better preserve our food for tomorrow.

A decor design inspired by gondola in Venice

people liked this gondola

These photos I took at Venice perfectly reflect where I got inspirations for this artwork design. Both the elegant shape of Gondola, as well as the classical structure of roof beams in traditional buildings, left a strong impression of this famous Italian city and its culture. I then realized that these two elements can be somehow fused in a way, to be used as an aesthetic reference for artwork design. With the help of 3D CAD software, SolidWorks, together with my modeling experiences, I finally found a solution to have these two elements harmoniously presented in one piece, although the process was pretty challenging and time consuming. You can see my effort and result from the photos of 3D printed parts below. During this project, my skill of 3D modeling has been trained, my understanding of aesthetics become deeper, my craftsmanship of sanding and gluing 3D printed parts has been improved, and most importantly, as I described in the statement of purpose, at the first time I realized that the latent power of introducing information technology into traditional product design field. From that time I started to think about moving into the technology world of Information Science and Human- Computer Interaction.
